Alright, here's my brutally honest ranking of all 14 Death Grips releases from worst to best: 14. Fashion Week: This is a purely instrumental release, and it falls flat without Ride's vocals. It's boring and adds nothing to their discography. 13. Gmail and the Restraining Orders: Just as uninspired as Fashion Week, but has the added "bonus" of being so short it's pretty much useless. 12. Death Grips EP: It's a rough start. The production is amateurish, and the songs lack the intensity and experimentation that would define their later work. It's not terrible, but definitely their weakest offering. 11. Interview 2016: Some cool ideas here, but it feels more like an experiment than a fully realized project. The ambient sections drag on, and the overall impact is minimal. 10. Steroids (Crouching Tiger Hidden Gabber Megamix): Overlong, exhausting, and ultimately not very memorable. There are moments of brilliance, but it's buried under layers of noise. 9. Government Plates: This album is weird and experimental, but not in a good way. Some of the production choices are grating, and the songs are often unfocused. It's a mess. 8. No Love Deep Web: Controversial placement, I know. But I feel the production is too muddy and the songs are less impactful compared to their other work. The novelty of the album cover has worn off. 7. Niggas on the Moon: The Björk samples are interesting, but they don't always mesh well with Death Grips' sound. The songs are decent, but this half of The Powers That B is weaker than Jenny Death. 6. Bottomless Pit: While still pretty good, it sounds more like a rehash of previous sounds rather than pushing the envelope. 5. Year of the Snitch: A return to form after Bottomless Pit. More experimental and out there. Still, not among their best. 4. The Powers That B: As a whole, it's a masterpiece. It has the experimental Niggas on the Moon, and also Jenny Death which could easily be their best. 3. Jenny Death: I love how fast and raw it is. 2. The Money Store: Pure energy. This is Death Grips at their most accessible, without sacrificing any of their intensity. Every track is a banger. 1. Exmilitary: This mixtape is a masterpiece. The raw energy, the aggressive vocals, the experimental production – it's all perfect. This is Death Grips at their most innovative and influential. It's their best work.
