Alright, here's my brutally honest ranking of all 21 Michael Jackson albums, from worst to best. Buckle up, some of these might sting: 1. Farewell My Summer Love: This is bottom of the barrel. A cash grab of outtakes and remixes. It's clearly unfinished and lacks any real artistic merit. Honestly, it's an insult to his legacy. 2. Looking Back to Yesterday: Another collection of leftovers. Predictable and formulaic. Jackson's vocals are fine, but the material is weak and uninspired. 3. E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial: I hate this album with burning passion. It is just him reading off of a movie script. 4. Got to Be There: While not terrible, it's a very basic, generic early 70s Motown sound. He was just a kid, and it shows. Lacks any real personality. 5. Ben: Slightly better than "Got to Be There," but still sounds like a kids' project. The title track is good, but the rest is forgettable. 6. Music & Me: More of the same. Jackson's voice is developing, but the material is still mostly fluff. It's just there. 7. Forever, Michael: He's getting a bit better here. Still very much a kid-friendly sound, but hints of the artist he would become are starting to peek through. 8. Michael: This album is posthumous, and it sounds like it. The production is all over the place, and the authenticity of some tracks is questionable. It's a sad reminder of what could have been. 9. Blood on the Dancefloor: HIStory in the Mix: Some okay remixes and a handful of new tracks that are far from his best. The title track is cool, but the rest is filler. 10. Thriller 25: It has too many remixes. The original album is great (as you'll see later), but the remixes are unnecessary and ruin the flow. 11. This Is It: This album is a decent compilation of his hits, but the new track "This Is It" is forgettable. 12. Bad 25: Slightly better than the original album, some fun extra tracks and live performances. 13. Immortal: A Cirque du Soleil soundtrack. It's a cool concept, but it doesn't hold up as a cohesive album. Fun for fans, but not essential. 14. XSCAPE: Another posthumous release. It's better than "Michael" because the tracks are more polished, but it still feels incomplete. 15. Thriller 40: Better than the original album as it's a complete package. 16. Invincible: Overlong and inconsistent. Some great tracks, but weighed down by too much filler. It tried to recapture the "Dangerous" magic, but falls short. 17. HIStory: Past, Present and Future, Book I: Ambitious and sprawling. The "HIStory Begins" disc is fantastic, but the "HIStory Continues" disc is a mixed bag. Still, a bold and important work. 18. Dangerous: A huge leap forward. Grittier and more experimental than "Bad." Some of his best singles are on this album. 19. Bad: A pop masterpiece. Every track is a banger. Polished, slick, and undeniably catchy. 20. Off the Wall: The album that launched him into superstardom. Funky, disco-infused, and full of energy. It's the sound of a young artist finding his voice. 21. Thriller: No explanation needed. The perfect pop album. Groundbreaking, iconic, and timeless. Every song is a classic. There you go. My honest, unfiltered take. Feel free to disagree.
