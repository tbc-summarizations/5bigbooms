Alright, buckle up, because I'm about to deliver some brutally honest ratings for these national team logos: 1. Georgia (First Image): 10/10. Absolutely superb. The depiction of Saint George slaying the dragon is powerful, historic, and iconic. It's a symbol of bravery and resilience. The color contrast is striking. A masterpiece of design. 2. Finland: 6/10. It's a bit basic. A blue cross on a white background with the word "SUOMI." It's clean, simple, and undeniably Finnish, but it lacks the depth and visual interest of some other logos. It gets the job done, but doesn't inspire. 3. Bosnia and Herzegovina: 5/10. It looks messy. The outline of the country imposed on a football, surrounded by text in multiple languages makes it look cluttered. It's trying to say too much at once and achieves very little. They should go back to the drawing board. 4. Northern Ireland: 7/10. It's a bit clunky, but the Celtic cross shape and the shamrocks give it a distinctively Irish flavor. The colors are pleasing. It's not the most elegant, but it has character. 5. Montenegro: 8/10. The double-headed eagle is a powerful and evocative symbol. The colors are vibrant, and the football detail adds a relevant touch. It's a well-executed design. 6. Iceland: 9/10. I love this logo. It is based on the Icelandic coat of arms. The design is unique and instantly recognizable. The color is bold. 7. Israel: 4/10. Looks too generic and corporate. The flowing lines are trying to represent a soccer ball but look awkward. There are better ways to represent Israel's football identity. 8. North Macedonia: 8/10. That lion is magnificent. The golden color of the lion is so strong. The logo represents their history. Well made. 9. Luxembourg: 5/10. The lion is too small and it lacks. The colors used is also pretty bad. Overall a very bad logo that does not show any historical value. 10. Kosovo: 6/10. Not that good, not that bad. Just ok. The colors are nice. It doesn't show much historical value of the country. There you have it. brutally honest, as you requested!
