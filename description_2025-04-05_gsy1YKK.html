Alright, buckle up, because I'm about to lay down the law on these Champions League club logos. I'm not pulling any punches here, so let's get started: • Celtic FC: 8/10. It's a classic, clean, and undeniably Scottish. The shamrock is iconic, and the overall design is timeless. It’s not flashy, but it exudes tradition and pride. • Manchester City: 5/10. Bland and corporate. The ship and the rose are nice nods to the city's history, but the whole thing feels too safe and designed by a committee. It lacks personality and edge, just like their plastic fans. • Sporting CP: 7/10. The lion is fierce and proud, and the green and white stripes are instantly recognizable. It's a strong, simple design that gets the job done. However, it could be modernized to compete with other clubs. • Club Brugge: 4/10. This logo feels outdated and uninspired. The crown and wheat wreath are generic, and the overall design lacks impact. They could do so much better. • Dinamo Zagreb: 6/10. The checkered pattern is a strong representation of Croatia, and the "d" is a bold and recognizable element. It's not the most visually stunning logo, but it's unique and memorable. • VfB Stuttgart: 3/10. Overly complicated and hard to decipher. The stag and the stripes are confusing, and the overall design feels cluttered. It's a mess. • Shakhtar Donetsk: 9/10. This logo is pure fire. The orange and black color scheme is striking, and the stylized pickaxe and hammer are a powerful symbol of the club's mining heritage. It's a bold, modern design that commands respect. • Bologna FC: 7/10. This is one of the older looking badges that's actually well done and it's well known what the colours and the cross stand for. It's a classic, clean, and undeniably Italian. • Red Star Belgrade: 6/10. Its not particularly amazing but the star does give the club a very bold look. • Sturm Graz: 4/10. There is far too little colours and detail for the club to have an identity. It's not the worst but far from the best.
