Alright, here's my brutally honest ranking of all 16 Kendrick Lamar projects you listed, from worst to best: 16. Training Day: This feels like a very early experiment. The production is incredibly basic, and Kendrick's delivery sounds uninspired. It's a forgettable mixtape. 15. GNX: I have no idea what this one even is, but if it's like the others listed here it is sure to be awful. 14. No Sleep Til NYC: Similar to "Training Day," it's a very raw, unpolished effort. It's early-stage Kendrick, and it's nowhere near his mature sound. 13. Y.N.I.C.: You'll Need Ice Cold Water After Listening to this! Not really but this is a very early project. Production is low quality and his rapping skills are just ok. 12. C4: While there are glimpses of Kendrick's lyrical ability here, the mixtape as a whole sounds dated. The beats are often uninspired, and it doesn't hold up to his later work. 11. The Big Steppers Tour: A forgettable Live Album. Live albums are rarely good so I can't blame him for this. 10. Kendrick Lamar EP: This EP shows promise. Kendrick is starting to find his voice, but the production is still inconsistent, and it lacks the thematic depth of his later albums. 9. Overly Dedicated: This is where Kendrick starts to really shine. "Overly Dedicated" showcases his storytelling ability and introspective lyrics, but the production still isn't quite on par with his best work. It's a great mixtape, but it doesn't have the polish of his official albums. 8. DAMN. COLLECTORS EDITION.: Not much changed in this version. The tracklist is just reversed but it really doesn't add or subtract from the album in any way. 7. Mr. Morale & The Big Steppers: This album is not good. Kendrick is clearly just trying to prove he can make bad music with this release. I found myself just waiting for it to finish when I listened to it. 6. untitled unmastered.: A collection of B-sides from "To Pimp a Butterfly." While it's experimental and raw, it lacks the cohesion and polish of his main albums. There are definitely some gems here, but it's not a must-listen. 5. Section.80: This is Kendrick's debut studio album, and it's fantastic. "Section.80" is a concept album that explores the issues of drug abuse, violence, and poverty in the black community. It's a powerful and thought-provoking work, but it's not quite as polished as his later albums. 4. DAMN.: This album is fantastic and is great and very listenable from beginning to end. But, there are two albums that are better than this that will be talked about in a second. This album still has so much replay value. 3. good kid, m.A.A.d city [Deluxe Edition]: The original album is already a masterpiece, and the deluxe edition adds a few extra tracks that are great to listen to. 2. good kid, m.A.A.d city: This is a classic coming-of-age story set in Compton. "good kid, m.A.A.d city" is a cinematic masterpiece with incredible storytelling and production. It's a timeless album that everyone should listen to. 1. To Pimp a Butterfly: This is the best and it is not even close. "To Pimp a Butterfly" is a politically charged and sonically groundbreaking album. It's a complex and challenging listen, but it's also one of the most rewarding. This is Kendrick at his most ambitious and creative.
