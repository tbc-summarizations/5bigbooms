Alright, here’s my brutally honest ranking of The Beatles' albums, from worst to best: 1. Yellow Submarine: This is just a sad cash-grab. A few good songs, but mostly filler and orchestral garbage. The Beatles clearly didn’t care about this one. 2. The Beatles at the Hollywood Bowl: Live albums are okay, but this is just a bunch of screaming and not much else. The sound quality is awful. 3. On Air – Live at the BBC, Volume 2: More live stuff, but still suffers from poor audio and a lack of originality. It's like they're just going through the motions. 4. Get Back: The Rooftop Performance: A cool historical footnote, but not something I'd listen to for enjoyment. The sound is rough, and it's just a brief moment in time. 5. Anthology 1: An interesting historical document, but too much rough stuff to be truly enjoyable. It's like listening to unfinished ideas. 6. Let It Be... Naked: Slightly better than the original due to less Phil Spector interference, but still a flawed album. It’s an improvement, but not a huge one. 7. Love (Remixes): This is just weird. Some of the mashups are okay, but overall, it’s a pointless exercise. It ruins the original songs. 8. Anthology 2: More demos and outtakes that don't quite measure up. It's for hardcore fans only. 9. Anthology 3: Even further down the rabbit hole of demos and alternate takes. Getting pretty boring. 10. Beatles For Sale: Some good tracks, but overall, a bit of a slump after their initial explosion. It feels rushed. 11. With The Beatles: A step up from their debut, but still early days. The songwriting isn’t quite there yet. 12. Past Masters, Volume One: A collection of singles and B-sides. It's okay, but nothing special. 13. Past Masters, Volume Two: More of the same. Not essential listening. 14. Live at the BBC: A decent live album, but not as essential as their studio work. It’s just a collection of covers and early hits. 15. Help!: A mixed bag. Some great songs, but also some forgettable ones. It's a bit uneven. 16. Please Please Me: A solid debut album with energy, but their songwriting is still developing. A classic start. 17. Let It Be: Overproduced and depressing, but it has some moments of brilliance. It's a sad farewell. 18. Magical Mystery Tour: Some great songs mixed with silly novelty tracks. It's a fun but uneven trip. 19. A Hard Day's Night: Pure early Beatles energy. Catchy and fun, but a bit one-dimensional. 20. The Beatles: The White Album is way too long and self-indulgent, but some amazing songs scattered throughout. It needs heavy editing. 21. Abbey Road: Polished and professional, but a bit too safe. It's a great album, but lacks the raw creativity of their best work. 22. Rubber Soul: A transitional album where their songwriting starts to get more complex and interesting. A solid effort. 23. Revolver: A bold and experimental album with some of their most innovative songs. It's pushing boundaries. 24. Sgt Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band: This is The Beatles at their peak. Revolutionary, creative, and groundbreaking. A true masterpiece.
