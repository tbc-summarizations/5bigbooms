Alright, here's my brutally honest ranking of all Drake albums, mixtapes, and EPs, from worst to best: 1. $ome $exy $ongs 4 U: This is something I'd never play again. The production is so bad it is as if a child with learning difficulties made this in his mom's basement. 2. Room for Improvement: This is where Drake started, and it shows. 3. 100 GIGS (EP): Utter garbage! A compilation of throwaway tracks and bad ideas. 4. For All The Dogs Scary Hours Edition: If you like dogs barking in the background, listen to this. 5. Dark Lane Demo Tapes: It feels unfinished, and the tracklist is all over the place. 6. Honestly, Nevermind: I actually tried to enjoy this, but it is too difficult. 7. For All the Dogs: Drake is repeating himself and trying out the same flows he always tries. It sounds repetitive. 8. Certified Lover Boy: This album had so much hype, but it failed to deliver. 9. Scary Hours (EP): The original Scary Hours was underwhelming. 10. What a Time to Be Alive: It feels more like a Future project with Drake features than a true collab. 11. Views: A bloated, inconsistent album that tries to be everything and ends up being nothing. 12. Scorpion: The "A-side/B-side" structure feels disjointed. 13. So Far Gone (EP): It lacks the charm and raw emotion of the original mixtape. 14. Scary Hours 2 (EP): A slight improvement over the original, but still not essential. 15. The Best in the World Pack (EP): Decent tracks. 16. Thank Me Later: It's a bit too polished and lacks the personality of his later work. 17. More Life: It's a playlist masquerading as an album, but it's still pretty good. 18. Comeback Season: This is where Drake really started to find his voice. 19. Her Loss: A solid collaboration with 21 Savage. 20. If You're Reading This It's Too Late: It's dark, brooding, and full of energy. 21. So Far Gone (Mixtape): This mixtape is iconic. It's raw, emotional, and full of bangers. 22. Care Package: A collection of loose tracks and deep cuts that showcase Drake's versatility. 23. Take Care: It's dark, introspective, and full of memorable moments. 24. Nothing Was the Same: This is Drake at his most confident and introspective. The production is lush, the lyrics are sharp, and the overall vibe is impeccable.
