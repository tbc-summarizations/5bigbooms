Alright, here's my completely subjective and unfiltered ranking of Tyler, The Creator's albums and related releases, from worst to best: 1. Live At Splash!: This is a live performance, and honestly, it's just not a great listening experience. Live albums often fall flat, and this one is no exception. 2. Bastard: It's got that raw, shock-value energy, but it's also incredibly immature and often offensive for the sake of being offensive. It's a relic of a different time and doesn't hold up well. 3. Goblin: Another album filled with shock value. Tyler was clearly just trying to get people to talk about him and make people uncomfortable and honestly didn't care about the music. 4. Music Inspired by Illumination & Dr. Seuss' The Grinch: While I respect Tyler's experimentation, this soundtrack is simply not as enjoyable as his main albums. It feels a bit too safe and family-friendly. 5. CHROMAKOPIA: This is an unreleased album, and I can see why it was scrapped. It just doesn't have the same energy as his other work. 6. IGOR: The Estate Sale: This release is simply weaker than the original IGOR. The songs removed were removed for a reason. 7. CHROMAKOPIA: The Estate Sale: This version of the unreleased album still does not beat the original album. 8. CALL ME IF YOU GET LOST: The Estate Sale: While it adds some interesting tracks, the main album is already so strong that the extras don't elevate it significantly. 9. Wolf: There are some great tracks on Wolf, but it's overall inconsistent. You can sense Tyler starting to evolve, but it hasn't fully gelled yet. 10. Cherry Bomb: Controversial pick, but Cherry Bomb is a messy masterpiece. The production is abrasive, but there are moments of brilliance that hint at the artist Tyler would become. It's a bold and unapologetic album. 11. CALL ME IF YOU GET LOST: A solid project from top to bottom. There are so many bangers and great lyrics. 12. Flower Boy: This is where Tyler truly blossomed. Flower Boy is introspective, musically lush, and emotionally resonant. It's a beautiful album that signaled a major turning point in his career. 13. IGOR: In my opinion, IGOR is Tyler's magnum opus. It's a cohesive, conceptual album with incredible production, songwriting, and emotional depth. It's a unique and unforgettable listening experience.
