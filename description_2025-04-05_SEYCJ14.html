Alright, here's my brutally honest ranking of all 42 MF DOOM albums, from worst to best. No holding back, just my straight-up opinion: 1. Expektoration Live: Live albums are rarely essential, and this one doesn't change that. The energy's there, but the sound quality is meh, and it's a one-time listen. 2. Special Herbs & Spices Vol. 1: Some interesting sounds but it fails to stand out. 3. Live from Planet X: Another live album, and it just doesn't capture the magic of DOOM's studio work. Skip it. 4. Special Herbs, Vol. 1: Instrumental albums usually only appeal to a select audience, and even then, they are rarely that good. 5. Take Me to Your Leader: King Ghidora has a few good songs but is not close to the worst album on the list. 6. Four Tet Remixes: Remixes can be cool, but these don't add much. The original tracks are superior. 7. Special Herbs, Vol. 2: More instrumental beats. Some are decent, but it's mostly background music. 8. Special Herbs, Vol. 3: Still more instrumentals, and this one doesn't offer much in the way of innovation. 9. Special Herbs, Vol. 4: The beats start to blend together. It's hard to distinguish one from another. 10. Rae Dawn / Change the Beat: This one is just a single, and not a very memorable one at that. 11. MM..LeftOvers: As the title implies, these are leftovers. Some interesting snippets, but mostly forgettable. 12. Venomous Villain: The concept is interesting, but the execution falls flat. It's a bit too chaotic and unfocused. 13. Occult Hymn: Experimental, but not in a good way. This one is a bit too out there for most listeners. 14. Special Herbs 7,8: Some interesting loops, but it's nothing you haven't heard before. 15. Special Herbs 9+0: More of the same. Decent instrumentals, but nothing groundbreaking. 16. Super What?: Lyrically weak, and the production is uninspired. This is a serious low point in DOOM's discography. 17. Special Blends 1+2: Decent beats, but it doesn't stand out from the rest of the "Special Herbs" series. 18. Czarface Meets Metal Face: The potential was there, but the collaboration doesn't fully gel. It's decent, but not great. 19. MUGGS x DOOM: The production is solid, but DOOM's rhymes feel a bit phoned in. 20. NehruvianDOOM: Some good moments, but it's not as strong as other collaborative efforts. 21. King Ghidra: This is MF DOOM's persona but a lower point in his discography. 22. Man's Worst Enemy: More MF DOOM personas but a lower point in his discography. 23. Black Bastards: Controversial name aside, this album has some solid tracks but a lower point in his discography. 24. MF: The album is just called MF and only includes instrumentals. 25. Special Herbs, Vols. 1 & 2: Some people find the instrumentals relaxing or creative, but they're just not my cup of tea. 26. Special Herbs, Vols. 3 & 4: The instrumentals are only getting old. 27. Special Herbs, Vols. 5 & 6: Some better instrumentals on this one. 28. Special Herbs, 4,5,6: It's fine but it would be better if it had DOOM rapping over it. 29. Madvillainy 2 The Madlib Remix: An interesting remix, but some of the charm of the original is lost. 30. Mr. Hood: A solid start to DOOM's career, but it doesn't quite reach the heights of his later work. 31. The Prof Meets The Supervillain: The Prof is a great producer with a distinct sound but there are better MF DOOM albums. 32. Vaudeville Villain: This is one of his best and more well-known albums, but not even close to the best of his discography. 33. Bookhead: Bookhead is not his best but better than some other albums on this list. 34.
